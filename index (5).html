La Oportunidad de Entrada

Sin marca OEM genuina
Todas las marcas dominantes (Bera, EK, Toro, AVA, Escuda) usan CKD chino sin afiliación OEM. Ninguna ofrece una garantía extendida y respaldada por un fabricante global. Hero entraría como el primer OEM indio con garantía de 4 años — una promesa verificable, no comercial.
TVS valida el modelo
Servisuministros JPG opera ensamblaje CKD en Anzoátegui bajo MEIV. La ruta regulatoria está probada. Las piezas de OEM indio pasan por la aduana venezolana. Hero replica un modelo probado.
Economía de delivery impulsando la demanda
A medida que la economía venezolana se recupera, el consumidor con mayor poder adquisitivo buscará naturalmente un producto de mayor calidad — ese es el ciclo natural del mercado. Hero estará posicionado en ese momento exacto: cuando Bera y Toro siguen siendo más baratos, Hero ofrecerá repuestos garantizados, calidad OEM y servicio de clase mundial, cerrando una brecha que hoy no tiene quien la llene. A esto se suma el crecimiento estructural del delivery por aplicaciones y la logística de última milla, que ya hoy impulsa la demanda. Los operadores buscan marcas confiables con repuestos disponibles — exactamente el posicionamiento de Hero con su garantía de 4 años.

Legal · Fiscal · Regulatory · Venezuela 2025–2026

Two-Wheeler Import & Ensamblaje Framework

Full regulatory picture for importing or assembling motorcycles in Venezuela — tariffs, IVA, MEIV assembly regime, technical standards, and how everything changed from early 2025 to today.

⚠
Regulatory Volatility — Critical Context
Venezuela's automotive tariff framework has been amended at least 9 times since April 2024 (April 2024 base decree + 8 partial reforms through January 2026). Duty rates, IVA exemptions, and procedural requirements have all changed mid-year without notice. Every financial model must scenario-plan at multiple tariff levels. No contract should be structured assuming current rates are permanent.

The domestic content discount explained: The 4% duty reduction (20% → 16%) is the government's reward for buying Venezolano parts. The parts don't need to be technically complex — Motos Toro's deal covers cables, seats, tires, plastics, and basic metal brackets sourced from 9 Venezolano SMEs represented by APYME. The $5M+ is Motos Toro's procurement commitment to those suppliers, not a capital investment in a factory. At 5,000 units/year, the 4% duty saving = ~$100–120/unit = ~$500–600K annual cost advantage vs. a competitor without local content. Over time, this is not optional — it is a structural margin advantage.

2. What Changed — Early 2025 to Today

Two separate government decisions — duty hike + IVA elimination — roughly doubled the cost de getting a motorcycle into Venezuela in under 12 meses.

Early 2025 — Baseline
CKD duty ~3% · IVA 0% on CKD · Carga total on CIF: ~3%
A motorcycle with CIF value de $500 landed at ~$515. Retail price (with distributor + dealer margin): $800–$900. Strong assembly incentive — the duty differential between CBU and CKD was enormous. Todos 15+ assemblers were operating under this regime.
Mid-2025 — First Shock
CKD duty raised to 20% · IVA still 0% on CKD · Carga total: ~20%
Government issued a decree raising CKD motorcycle parts duty from ~3% to 20%. Domestic content path kept at 16% (4% reward for local sourcing). IVA exemption remained in force at this stage. Immediate retail impact: $800–900 → $2,000+. Industry projected 50% YoY sales volume decline by end 2025. Note: the duty reduction to 16% con contenido nacional was simultaneously introduced as the carrot.
June 30, 2025 — Second Shock
IVA exemption on CKD eliminated · 16% IVA now applies to CKD · Carga total: ~40%
Decree 5,147 (Gaceta Oficial 6,918 Extraordinario) eliminated the IVA exemption on CKD motorcycle parts (headings 9801–9807 de Chapter 98). The IVA de 16% now applies on top de the 20% duty. Heading 9808 (CBU fully assembled vehicles) was also deleted as a special category — CBU now taxed at full duty + full IVA with no special regime. The $5 landed on a $500 CIF unit is now ~$700. This was the most damaging change — it compressed the CBU vs CKD advantage significantly.
November 2025 — Motos Toro Deal
First major domestic content agreement signed under new regime
Ministry de Industries signed agreement with Motos Toro (Consorcio Ja-Na, Guacara, Carabobo). Deal: 2,284,000 domestic parts to be sourced from 9 Venezolano SMEs for 108,000 motorcycles assembled — ~21 parts per bike. Investment commitment: $5M+. Parts covered: cables, seats, tires, plastics, basic metal brackets. This qualifies Motos Toro for the 16% duty rate. Government (Minister Alex Saab) publicly invited all assemblers to follow this model. Motos Toro uses Loncin engines + Zongshen/Cyclone CKD from China.
December 31, 2025 / January 2026 — Procedural Tightening
Legal Regime 9 added as mandatory prerequisite for MEIV · No new rate changes
The partial reform de December 31, 2025 (effective February 3, 2026) added Legal Regime 9 as a required step before MPPIPN can issue any MEIV qualification. This adds a bureaucratic gate — assembler must first obtain Legal Regime 9 clearance, then apply for MEIV qualification, then obtain SENIAT authorization. Longer lead times for new entrants. No change to duty or IVA rates.

3. The MEIV Regime — How Ensamblaje Actually Works

MEIV = Material de Ensamblaje Importado para Vehículos. This is Venezuela's customs regime for importing vehicle parts for local assembly. It is the mandatory legal path for any CKD assembler.

Paso-by-step process (post Jan 2026)
1Register company as assembler with MPPIPN (Ministry de Popular Power for Industries and National Production). Requires physical facility, production plan, capacity certification.
2Obtain Legal Regime 9 clearance (added Dec 2025 reform). New bureaucratic gate required before MPPIPN can proceed.
3Submit detailed application to MPPIPN including technical specs, part lists, commercial data. MPPIPN issues the MEIV Qualification ("Oficio de Calificación").
4MEIV Qualification submitted to SENIAT to obtain the formal import authorization under the MEIV regime.
✓Import CKD parts under MEIV at reduced duty (20%, or 16% con contenido nacional prode). Assemble locally. SENIAT monitors compliance.
Key operational realities
Timeline: 12–18 meses for a new entrant to complete registration and first authorized import. Existing assemblers already in the system avoid this delay.
Reactivation shortcut: Companies in "reactivation or expansion" mode can temporarily import at a lower disassembly level under a 1-year provisional authorization (extendable 6 meses). Relevant for Hero if partnering with an existing registered assembler (e.g. an existing registered assembler).
Per-model scope: MEIV authorization covers specific part lists per motorcycle model. Adding a new model = new application.
Fast path: Partner with an existing registered assembler for contract CKD assembly. The distributor (Apex) holds the Hero distribution mandate. This bypasses the 12–18 month MPPIPN registration timeline.

5. Technical & Quality Standards — COVENIN / INTT

Two separate regulatory bodies govern technical compliance. COVENIN sets the product standards; INTT enforces homologation (type approval) before any model can be sold.

INTT — Mandatory Equipment (Todos Motorcycles in Circulation)
Braking — Legal minimumStop within 5 meters at 30 km/h. No ABS required at any engine size. No dual-circuit requirement. Basic drum brakes easily meet this. Every Hero model exceeds this standard by a wide margin.
Front lighting2 front headlights capable de identifying the vehicle at 150 meters distance. No minimum lumen requirement stated — visibility is the standard. No LED mandate.
Rear lighting1 red rear light visible at 100 meters at night. Plus 2 red rear reflectors indicating maximum vehicle width (for vehicles with more than 2 wheels — scooters/3W).
Mirrors2 mirrors — one right, one left. Standard on all production motorcycles.
Exhaust1 silencer device dampening motor explosions. No specific decibel limit in published regulation — must have a silencer fitted and functional.
Horn1 functional horn required.
CapacityMaximum 2 persons including the rider. Standard configuration on all production bikes.
RegistrationMust be registered in INTT's National Vehicle Registry before first sale. VIN pre-registration by the assembler/importer; dealer completes at point de sale.
INTT — Prototype Homologation (Per New Model)

One-time type approval per model before any units can be sold. Certificate covers all subsequent units de that model. Budget 3–6 meses, $5–15K per model.

Hero's export documentation package already used for Colombia, Ecuador, and other LATAM markets is directly reusable with translation and minor localization.

COVENIN mandatory compliance		 SENCAMER For imports subject to mandatory COVENIN norms or Technical Regulations, the importer must present the SENCAMER Registration Certificate alongside the customs declaration. This confirms the product meets applicable Venezolano technical standards. LOW
Bureaucratic but not a technical barrier. Obtain once per product category. Hero's Colombian distributor may already have partially completed this documentation.
National Content Political Pressure
Import substitution policy		 MPPIPN / Ministry Government has made clear it wants assemblers to incorporate Venezolano-made parts. The Motos Toro deal (Nov 2025) is being publicly promoted as the model to follow. Minister Alex Saab has explicitly invited all assemblers to replicate it. While not legally mandated (beyond the 16% vs 20% duty incentive), failure to engage with this agenda risks being singled out for tighter enforcement or future duty increases. MEDIUM — Strategic
Plan domestic content partnership from Year 1. Even a minimal program (cables, seats, basic plastics from Venezolano SMEs) buys political goodwill + the 16% duty rate. Budget $1–2M procurement commitment over 3 años to qualify. Align with Motos Toro's playbook — don't reinvent.
Country de Origen
Preferential trade agreements		 SENIAT / Customs No origin restrictions block Indian or Chino motorcycles. Andean Community (Colombia, Ecuador, Bolivia) goods receive preferential tariffs — not relevant for Hero (India-origin). Venezuela-China tariff negotiation underway (Ministerio de Comercio confirming negotiations for zero tariff on 400 products). If this progresses, Chino CKD kits may face lower duties than Indian — watch this closely. LOW now / Watch
VZ-China 0-tariff negotiation is real but timeline uncertain. If enacted, Indian brands lose ~4% duty advantage vs Chino. Monitor via Venezolano commerce ministry announcements.
