Unit Economics — Preliminary

CIF price (India → VZ)~$600–800 Splendor / Eco CBU. ~$400–550 CKD kit (Year 2). TVS CKD in Anzoátegui = the reference agreement to mirror for Hero assembly.
Import duty~30–40% CBU. CKD reduces to ~15–20%. All main VZ assemblers (Bera, EK, TVS) use CKD to capture this advantage. Hero CKD = Year 2 priority.
Retail target (VZ)Eco 100: $1,100–1,300 · Splendor Xpro: $1,500–1,700 · Hunk 160R: $2,600–3,000 · Xpulse 200 4V: $3,200–3,800. Quality + 4yr warranty positioning vs Chinese generics — not a price war.
Gross margin18–25% LATAM standard. $1,400 avg × 4,000 units Y1 × 20% = $1.12M gross. Y3 at 10,000 units = $2.8M. Add parts margin ~20% on top.
Break-even$300–500K launch (inventory + parts depot + dealer setup + marketing). Month 14–18 at 300–400 units/month. Sub-dealer network via Bera agents means units move through existing infrastructure from Day 1.
